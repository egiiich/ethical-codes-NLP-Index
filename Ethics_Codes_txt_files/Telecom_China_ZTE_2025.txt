Avoiding Insider Trading  Insider information refers to non-public information concerning the Company's operations or financial condition that could significantly impact its securities' market price. According to the Securities Law of the People's Republic of China, insider trading includes trading related securities, disclosing insider information, or advising others to trade securities before such information is public. As employees of a listed company, we must strictly comply with securities laws to prevent insider trading risks. We must remain vigilant, refraining from using insider information for securities trading or advising others to do so. Non-public information must be restricted to the minimum necessary recipients, with compliance ensured during information sharing. Particular caution is required in non-work settings, such as family gatherings or social interactions, where confidentiality obligations must be upheld. When asked about potential insider information, employees should firmly decline discussion and explain compliance requirements, safeguarding both the Company and personal integrity. Financial Information: Periodic reports, performance forecasts, preliminary earnings announcements, etc. Capital Operations: Mergers and acquisitions, significant investments, etc. Major Events: Significant litigation or arbitration, major asset disposals, etc. Other Information: Any information that may have a material impact on the company's securities prices.

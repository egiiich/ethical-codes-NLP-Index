# ESG controversies case mapping (APAC focus)

This file narrows controversy mapping to APAC-relevant cases and links them to the principle domains used in the thesis matrices.

## Case 1: Luckin Coffee (China, 2020) — investor trust and internal controls

Luckin Coffee’s accounting fraud case highlights the fragility of investor-facing ethics language when internal controls and escalation routines are weak. In matrix terms, this case maps most strongly to `company_to_investors`, `worker_to_manager`, and `hotline_principles`. The key analytical implication is that disclosures can look modern while evidence trails and internal challenge channels remain insufficient.

## Case 2: Rio Tinto / Juukan Gorge (Australia, 2020) — community impact and social license

The Juukan Gorge destruction case illustrates how gaps in stakeholder-sensitive governance can convert quickly into high-intensity legitimacy loss. In matrix terms, the case maps to `company_to_people`, `manager_to_worker`, `worker_to_manager`, and `hotline_principles`. The practical implication is that codes must include real escalation rights and accountability pathways when projects affect communities and heritage assets.

## Case 3: Toshiba accounting scandal (Japan, 2015) — governance pressure and speak-up constraints

The Toshiba case is a reference point for how pressure-driven management culture can distort reporting and suppress challenge behavior. In matrix logic, this is most relevant to `worker_to_manager`, `manager_to_worker`, `company_to_investors`, and `hotline_principles`. The core lesson is that formal compliance language must be paired with credible managerial accountability and upward voice protection.

## Case 4: POSCO governance controversies (Korea, 2021–2022 context) — anti-corruption and board-accountability signals

Governance disputes around anti-corruption and leadership accountability in major Korean corporates are useful reminders that process maturity can coexist with integrity risk. In the matrix, these concerns map to `company_to_investors`, `competitor_relations`, `hotline_principles`, and `manager_to_worker`. The implication is that APAC firms with relatively high formalization scores may still require stronger safeguards against enforcement and reputational shocks.

## Case 5: Supply-chain labor controversies in Southeast Asian manufacturing ecosystems

Recurring labor-rights and subcontracting controversies in Southeast Asian supply chains (including Indonesia-linked production networks) underscore that externalized operations remain a major ethics-risk frontier. These patterns map to `company_to_supplier`, `company_to_buyer`, `company_to_people`, and `worker_to_worker`. The lesson is that supplier governance must be treated as core ethics architecture, not peripheral CSR narrative.

## Synthesis of APAC controversy signals

Across APAC-oriented cases, three recurrent risk mechanisms appear:

1. Upward-voice deficits (`worker_to_manager`) weaken early warning quality.
2. Procedural compliance growth (`hotline_principles`) is necessary but not sufficient without managerial reciprocity.
3. External interfaces (`company_to_investors`, `company_to_supplier`, `company_to_people`) are high-cost failure zones when implementation depth lags behind policy text.

## Implication for thesis discussion

For the APAC-focused corpus, controversy interpretation should emphasize directional balance inside codes: whether employee obligations are matched by managerial accountability, whether investor-facing claims are supported by controls, and whether community/supply-chain sections are specific enough to support operational enforcement.
